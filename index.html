The Changing Landscape of Australian Car Sales

Explaining the Aussie Car Market

The Australian new car market has undergone fascinating changes in recent years. This visualisation aims to explore the recent evolution of new car sales in Australia. We will explore how sales have recovered from the COVID pandemic, both nation-wide and in each state and territory. We will also explore how Australians have adopting emerging technologies, and the rising brands and nationsin the industry.

How Have Sales Evolved in Australia?

The impacts of COVID-19 meant that 2020 saw the least new car sales in Australia since 2003. However, in the aftermath of the pandemic it took the nation just 1 year to return to pre-COVID sales levels. Using the slider to adjust the map, we can see how sales have evolved in each state. Victoria and Queensland have the most sales per capita, despite New South Wales having the most total sales. Interestingly, when considering the number of sales per 10,000 people, it took Victoria the longest to reach pre-COVID levels out of any state. Play with the slider and explore for yourself!

How Have the Cars Changed?

As electric vehicle technology continues to develop and become more accessible, Australians have adopted these vehicles at a growing rate. 6 years ago, less than 1% of new cars sold where Electric Vehicles (EVs). Now, 15% of new cars are EVs, and over a third of new cars sold in Australia this year are either EVs or Hybrid vehicles. Whilst traditional internal combustion engines (ICEs) still dominate, it's clear that Australians are keen to reap the benefits of modern EVs and Hybrid cars.
